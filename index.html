About Myself

I am Danny Harani, an aspiring software engineer and developer from Montreal, now living in Ottawa. I am in grade 12 at AY-Jackson secondary school. Computer programing has been an interest of mine for as long as I can remember and I started really looking into it when I was about 11 years old with basic block-type coding. When I was 14, I took it on to myslef to start learning to code. I used online resources, primarily youtube to learn coding on my own. I started with basic variable video's and learned up to loops. I then made simple input output type of games like hangman or rock paper scissors. When I felt confident in my abilities, I decided to learn python so I can diversify my knowledge in programing. However, I lost interest since I did not know what I should code, I had no inspiration. I took a break and decided to see what grade 10 compsci would teach me. I designed a simple game for little kids in elementary with actionscript and enjoyed the project. It boosted more of my creativity and I decided my break was over.

The year after, I learned C++ in grade 11, this programing language was more familiar to me than actionscript and I enjoyed it. This is where I am today, I am learning Javascript and the DOM through online resources like Khan academy and FreeCodeCamp. This portfolio will display 10 things I learned and why I enjoyed them. Here's a quick list of the coding languages I enjoyed learning (in no order):

  • Python
  • Javascript
  • Java
  • Kotlin
  • C++
